Grade 7 — Intro
What is the Heart?
Your body's most tireless machine
The heart is a hollow, muscular organ that acts as a pump — pushing blood throughout the entire body through blood vessels.
  • Located in the chest cavity, slightly left of midline
  • Size: roughly equal to your closed fist
  • Weight: approximately 250–350 grams in adults
  • Beats 100,000 times per day — ~70 per minute at rest
  • Protected by rib cage, enclosed in pericardium
teacher zone
Grade 7 — Concept
The Heart as a Pump
Why does the body need pumping?
  • Every cell needs oxygen and nutrients — heart delivers them via blood
  • Blood carries waste products (CO₂, urea) away from cells
  • Heart contracts and relaxes rhythmically — this is your heartbeat
  • Without circulation, cells would die within minutes
Heart → Arteries → Body Cells → Veins → Heart

Grade 10 — Anatomy
Detailed Structure
of the Heart
  • Wall has 3 layers: Epicardium · Myocardium · Endocardium
  • Septum — divides right and left sides completely
  • Tricuspid valve — right AV valve (3 cusps)
  • Bicuspid (Mitral) — left AV valve (2 cusps)
  • Semilunar valves — at exits into pulmonary artery & aorta
  • Chordae tendineae — strings preventing valve eversion
  • Left ventricle wall is 3× thicker than right

Grade 10 — Circulation
Double
Circulation
Blood passes through the heart twice per circuit
① Pulmonary
Right ventricle → Pulmonary artery → Lungs → Pulmonary vein → Left atrium
② Systemic
Left ventricle → Aorta → Body organs → Venae Cavae → Right atrium
  • Oxygenated and deoxygenated blood never mix
  • Pulmonary artery = deoxygenated · Pulmonary vein = oxygenated

Grade 10 — Cycle
Cardiac Cycle
— Basics
One complete heartbeat = one cardiac cycle. Consists of contraction (systole) and relaxation (diastole).
Atrial Systole → Ventricular Systole → Diastole → Repeat
  • Systole — heart muscle contracts, blood is pushed out
  • Diastole — heart muscle relaxes, chambers fill with blood
  • Duration of one cardiac cycle: 0.8 seconds at 75 bpm
  • Normal heart rate: 60–100 beats/min (resting adult)
  • Tachycardia → heart rate >100  |  Bradycardia → heart rate <60
teacher zone
Grade 10 — Blood Pressure
Blood Pressure
Force exerted by blood on vessel walls. Measured with a sphygmomanometer.
  • Systolic — during ventricular contraction. Normal: 120 mmHg
  • Diastolic — during ventricular relaxation. Normal: 80 mmHg
  • Written as 120/80 mmHg
  • Hypertension ≥ 140/90 · Hypotension < 90/60
  • Highest in aorta, drops progressively toward veins

Grade 11 — Conduction
Cardiac Conduction
System
The heart's own electrical wiring
  • SA Node — right atrium wall. The pacemaker. Generates impulse spontaneously.
  • AV Node — atria–ventricle junction. ~0.1 sec delay for atria to complete.
  • Bundle of His — through the septum to ventricles.
  • Bundle Branches — Right & Left along ventricular walls.
  • Purkinje Fibres — fine terminals causing simultaneous ventricular contraction.
  • Heart is myogenic — no nerve input needed to start

Grade 11 — Detailed Cycle
Cardiac Cycle
— Detailed Phases
Duration: 0.8 sec at 75 bpm
Phase Event Duration
Atrial SystoleAtria contract → blood into ventricles0.1 sec
Ventricular SystoleVentricles contract → blood into aorta & pulmonary artery0.3 sec
Joint DiastoleAll chambers relax → ventricles fill passively0.4 sec
  • Isovolumic contraction — ventricle contracts but volume unchanged (all valves shut)
  • Ejection phase — semilunar valves open, blood ejected out
  • Isovolumic relaxation — ventricle relaxes, all valves closed again
  • Filling phase — AV valves open, blood flows in passively (70%) + atrial kick (30%)
  • End Diastolic Volume (EDV): ~120 mL  |  End Systolic Volume (ESV): ~50 mL

Grade 11 — Sounds
Heart Sounds
"Lub — Dub"
"Lub" (S1)
Closure of AV valves (Mitral + Tricuspid) at start of ventricular systole. Longer, low-pitched.
"Dub" (S2)
Closure of Semilunar valves (Aortic + Pulmonary) at end of systole. Short, sharp.
  • S3, S4 = abnormal — heart failure / stiff ventricle
  • Murmurs — turbulent flow through leaky/stenotic valves
  • 4 auscultation points — fill during class

Grade 11 — Output
Cardiac Output &
Starling's Law
CO = Stroke Volume × Heart Rate
Normal: 70 mL × 75 bpm = 5.25 L/min
  • Stroke Volume (SV) — blood ejected per beat = EDV − ESV ≈ 70 mL
  • Ejection Fraction — SV/EDV × 100 = ~58–65% (normal). Below 40% = heart failure.
  • Starling's Law — "the more the heart is filled during diastole, the greater the force of contraction" (within limits)
  • During exercise CO can rise to 20–25 L/min in trained athletes
  • Preload — venous return (filling). Afterload — resistance heart pumps against.
  • Frank-Starling mechanism ensures both ventricles pump equal volumes despite variations

High Yield
Must-Know Facts
for NEET
  • Pulmonary artery carries deoxygenated blood; pulmonary vein carries oxygenated — most common trick question
  • SA node = pacemaker; inherent rate ~70–80 bpm; AV node backup = 40–60 bpm; Purkinje = 20–40 bpm
  • Heart is myogenic — impulse originates in cardiac muscle itself, not from the brain
  • Normal ECG: P→QRS→T. Atrial repolarisation hidden within QRS complex
  • Left ventricle wall thickness: ~10–15 mm; Right ventricle: ~3–5 mm
  • Chordae tendineae prevent AV valve eversion (not semilunar valves)
  • During ventricular systole, AV valves close (S1), semilunar valves open
  • During ventricular diastole, AV valves open, semilunar valves close (S2)
  • Coronary arteries supply blood to heart muscle itself — arise from base of aorta
  • Lymph from interstitial fluid → lymph capillaries → lymphatic vessels → subclavian vein

NEET 2019
MCQ — 01
Which of the following is incorrect regarding the cardiac cycle?

The duration of atrial systole, ventricular systole, and complete cardiac cycle are 0.1 sec, 0.3 sec, and 0.8 sec respectively.
(a) Duration of atrial systole = 0.1 sec — True
(b) Duration of ventricular systole = 0.3 sec — True
(c) Complete cardiac cycle = 0.8 sec — True
(d) All statements above are correct — This is NOT incorrect

Concept: cardiac cycle durations are standard board values — must be memorised exactly

NEET 2020
MCQ — 02
Which of the following statements about the SA node is correct?
(a) Located at the junction of the right atrium and right ventricle
(b) Acts as the pacemaker of the heart and generates impulses at ~70–75 per minute
(c) The impulse from SA node directly reaches Purkinje fibres without passing AV node
(d) SA node requires nerve impulse from the brain to initiate each heartbeat

SA node is in the right atrium wall (not AV junction). Heart is myogenic — no brain trigger needed.

NEET 2022
MCQ — 03
Identify the correct sequence of events in the cardiac conduction system:
(a) AV node → SA node → Bundle of His → Purkinje fibres
(b) SA node → AV node → Bundle of His → Bundle branches → Purkinje fibres
(c) SA node → Bundle of His → AV node → Purkinje fibres
(d) SA node → AV node → Purkinje fibres → Bundle of His

Sequence: SA → AV (delay) → Bundle of His → L+R branches → Purkinje. Order never changes.

NEET 2021
MCQ — 04
During a cardiac cycle, the "lub" sound is produced due to:
(a) Closure of semilunar valves at the end of ventricular systole
(b) Closure of bicuspid and tricuspid valves at the beginning of ventricular systole
(c) Opening of aortic and pulmonary valves
(d) Opening of mitral valve allowing blood into left ventricle

S1 (lub) = AV valves CLOSE. S2 (dub) = Semilunar valves CLOSE. Valves closing makes sound, not opening.
